The Portage Park Handyman

Rich Escallier, a Portage Park resident since 1988 and home handyman since 1978, offers a large range of indoor/ outdoor repairs and improvements. Because he is a self-employed, independent handyman, he can perform services at prices far below those of franchised handyman services. Rich brings over 25 years of experience in the home repair field. As a former owner of several apartment buildings, he is largely self-taught, learning the ins and outs of repairing and refurbishing buildings since 1978.

Rich has taught home repair and maintenance classes since 2006 as part of adult continuing education programs at the City Colleges of Chicago. Watch the Truman College course catalog for his classes.

His most extensive remodeling job was completed on his current residence, a 90- year-old Portage Park home which he refurbished from top to bottom, including building a second story addition.

Handyman Tips:

Did you know ...
the easiest way to clean windows is with a squeegee. Put the paper towels &spray bottles away. A squeegee, a scrubber or sponge, and a couple of clean rags or old towels will do the job quicker &easier. Mix 1 tbsp of dish washing soap (the pros use Dawn) with a gallon of water. If you have greasy dirty windows, you can add up to 1/2 cup of amonia per gallon of water.Technique = Wipe the squeegee with the towel after every pass. Wipe the perimeter of the window with your finger in a separate clean rag or towel as the last step. Overlap 2" between strokes.Don't lay the squeegee blade side down, & change the blade after a season or two.
